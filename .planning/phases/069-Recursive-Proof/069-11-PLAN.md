# 069-11 Recursive Proof Benchmarks And Measurements

<objective>
Measure real local Nova fold/compression/prove/verify and Plonky3 base/recursive
prove/verify behavior, proof/witness/statement/public-input sizes, peak memory,
and recovery cost on fixed fixtures. Measure the complete client-centric storage
and traffic budget: unique challenge-pack ingress, dedup/compression ratio,
90-day RS-sharded plateau, 16-shard COW state/snapshot growth/skew/reshard,
compact anchors, per-role network traffic, seed-recovery scan cost, rolling-
history cost and ciphertext-erasure-sharded Wallet Backup V5. Reserve explicit
`not_measured_phase071` mailbox rows without fabricating bytes, traffic, plateau,
or a positive admission cap.
Replace imported targets with reproducible local evidence or issue a justified
stop/split decision.

## capacity_budget_contract

Measurements MUST use exact persisted canonical bytes and publish both logical
and physical storage accounting:

- Time basis is five-second checkpoints: `17,280` blocks/day and `17.28`
  1000-block epochs/day. Calculations MUST be recomputed from configured cadence,
  never copied as constants without an assertion.
- `permanent_compact_history` includes only epoch anchors and their close
  certificates, MMR/history nodes,
  rotation commitments, and minimal committee/parameter manifests whose
  permanent verification need is explicitly justified—never per-checkpoint
  archive-manifest bodies. Target anchor is 1 KiB,
  hard cap is 4 KiB, and total permanent historical growth MUST be at most
  100 KiB/day. The 4 KiB object cap is an anti-amplification ceiling, not a
  capacity allowance: actual objects at the configured cadence MUST separately
  satisfy the stricter daily aggregate budget. Full proof bodies or challenge
  data in this class fail. At 100 KiB/day the historical cap is about 35.65
  MiB/year; the report MUST compute the exact configured-year value rather than
  round it into a capacity promise.
- `live_state` reports total and daily net additions/deletions for HJMT leaves,
  encrypted recovery data, tree nodes, and current/last-three COW snapshot chunks.
  It MUST also report all 16 shard ranges, three-replica/write-quorum overhead,
  bytes/leaves/chunks per placement and failure domain, max/median skew, route/
  manifest bytes, membership and all-shard seed-recovery scan/false-positive
  traffic, reshard COW copy/delta-catch-up/repair bytes and latency, and
  partition/restart backlog. It MUST NOT be called historical growth, assumed
  bounded by time, or measured as a monolithic full-state replica.
- `challenge_ring` reports raw unique bytes/day, dedup ratio, canonical binary
  size including non-epoch header/QC bodies, lossless compression ratio,
  90-day logical plateau, mandatory RS(10,16) physical size, per-watcher shard
  size, placement skew, reconstruction/repair bandwidth, and retrieval-audit
  bandwidth. The rejected three-full-copy physical size MAY be reported only as
  a labeled comparison and MUST NOT satisfy a gate. A size cap is a DoS limit,
  not a capacity estimate.
- `recursive` reports PP, PK, VK, running IVC/recovery state, base/intermediate/
  epoch/history proof bytes, setup/fold/compress/base/epoch/history prove and
  verify time/RSS, and security loss separately. Measure rolling successors at
  lengths 1, 2, 3, 10, and 100 and at the configured lifetime horizon. Report
  the concrete per-proof error bound, composition rule, accepted-epoch budget,
  inherited cumulative error, and residual security; do not copy the nominal
  `security_bits` field as a result. Stop if proof/verifier/recursion cost is not
  operationally bounded or the minimum residual target is missed.
- `nova_retention` reports state-by-state logical and physical bytes/object counts
  for `OpenEpoch`, body-less `GapRecorded`, `ClosedAwaitingPq`, `PqSuperseded`, `Abandoned`,
  `DeleteEligible`, and `Deleted`; two accepted bodies per epoch/eight pending
  epochs/16 bodies/2 MiB checked limits; temporary retry and post-CAS GC lag;
  normal-PQ time-to-retirement; outage time-to-backpressure; and abandonment/
  recovery behavior. It separately measures the active accumulator plus two
  recovery snapshots and journal overhead. Plan 11 MUST activate a new profile
  generation only with a positive finite `max_nova_hot_recovery_bytes` derived
  from identical backend/parameter fixtures plus stated safety margin. The
  24-GiB process RSS ceiling and 128-MiB per-block experiment cap are never
  storage capacity inputs.
- `network` reports bytes per role and cadence: aggregator-to-consensus,
  validator votes/QC, DA publication/reference, archive announce/fetch/repair,
  recursive-verifier VK once per generation, proof envelopes per epoch/on-demand,
  and wallet-specific receipts/witnesses. Fanout and cache hit/miss are explicit.
- `offline_mailbox` is a mandatory disposition row, not a Phase-069 benchmark.
  It records `status: not_measured_phase071`, semantic owner Phase 071, exact
  Phase-069 reserved registry/profile/manifest digests, `declared_only`, cap
  zero, and no-online-path evidence. Notice/ciphertext/activation/replica/ACK/GC
  sizes, entries/day, partition skew, physical plateau, traffic, and CPU fields
  MUST be null/absent with a typed `DeferredToPhase071` disposition—not zero as
  if measured. Plan 11 MUST NOT create a mailbox benchmark, derive a cap, mutate
  the reserved generation, or enable writes. The Phase-071 spec owns those real
  measurements and a later new-generation activation.
- `notary` measures close-anchor preparation/certificate bytes and CPU/latency
  with 0, 1, configured-limit, and limit+1 ready evidence-MMR appends. Limit+1
  queues the excess without changing close/finality latency semantics; any
  unbounded work or daily compact-growth breach is a stop/split.
- `wallet` reports finalized receipt size, local history growth, encrypted backup
  full/incremental ciphertext size, `k/n` shard/manifest overhead, upload/
  reconstruction/decrypt/read-back/CAS/restore time, KDF cost, retained-three
  logical-generation overhead, lost/corrupt-shard repair, and provider failure-
  domain retry traffic without exposing secrets.

Plan 06 cadence or Plan 09 redundancy/compression defaults MAY change only from
this complete ledger plus Plan 12 behavior evidence. A target miss must produce a
versioned decision or stop/split, never an unrecorded relaxed cap.
