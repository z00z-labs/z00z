# 069-12 Real-Backend Simulator And Cadence Evidence

<objective>
Run a deterministic local end-to-end scenario using real project primitives,
real Nova 3/5-step chains, a bounded real Plonky3 recursive epoch proof, and
height-999/1000 canonical epoch closure followed by asynchronous proof/history
publication. Separately run an accelerated 100-day retention lifecycle through
the real codecs and state machines. Prove the archive-window plateau,
mailbox-absent current-unspent seed recovery, wallet-owned history/backup
recovery, crash consistency, restart,
reorg, and resource bounds
without pretending that accelerated height/time is proof-throughput evidence or
giving recursive artifacts authority over finality.
</objective>

## acceptance_tests

- Real deterministic-input 3/5 Nova chains and bounded real Plonky3 epoch verify.
- Height 999 reports an open epoch. Height 1000 atomically commits a compact
  `EpochCloseAnchorV2` for the closed range after the canonical block's inherited
  DA-ready/QC gates; it MUST NOT then wait for Nova, Plonky3, PQ, or additional
  evidence publication. Recursive evidence is attached later
  through the evidence state machine; missing evidence can block pruning or
  evidence promotion, never canonical admission or finality.
- Real Plonky3 tests cover exact-epoch proof plus rolling-history base,
  successor, and parameter-rotation bridge. A predecessor digest without
  verification of the predecessor history proof MUST reject.
- An accelerated height/time retention scenario fills the 1,555,200-block
  (90-day at 5 seconds) window, advances through day 100, and proves retained
  challenge bytes plateau after expiry. It uses real codecs, manifests, ledger
  transitions, deletion authorization, and readback checks, but MUST NOT claim
  that 1,728,000 real proofs were generated or benchmarked.
- A separate accelerated Nova lifecycle keeps at most two accepted bodies per
  epoch, eight pending epochs, 16 bodies, and 2 MiB. With Plonky3 stopped, the
  ninth epoch still closes and folding continues while compression/publication
  returns `NovaEvidenceBackpressure` and the uncompressed epoch becomes terminal
  `GapRecorded` without a ticket or GC. Normal PQ coverage and explicit
  body-bearing abandoned-body retirement execute every ticket/head/unlink
  failpoint; neither changes
  challenge/current-state/anchor bytes.
- Permanent compact-anchor/MMR history grows at no more than 100 KiB/day and
  every `EpochCloseAnchorV2`, `EpochEvidenceAnchorV2`, and permanent epoch-close
  certificate is at most 4 KiB;
  current state and the selected live
  recovery snapshots are reported separately from historical growth.
- A wallet verifies `FinalizedWalletReceiptV2`, spends only against a fresh
  current-root witness, recovers current spendable outputs from seed by scanning
  all live shards, completes `WalletBackupV5` encrypt-before-erasure upload/
  `k`-of-`n` reconstruct/decrypt/readback/CAS head advancement, restores from an
  untrusted provider, and retains the latest three reconstructible immutable
  logical generations. The network does not retain that wallet's full
  receipt/history stream.
- Phase-069 scenario proves every reserved mailbox stage/decode/read/activate/
  ACK/GC path rejects before allocation/mutation and seed recovery succeeds with
  no mailbox implementation/data. Day 0/expiry/delivery/replica/ACK/GC scenarios
  are transferred to Phase 071 and MUST NOT be reported as executed here.
- Crash injection covers every boundary of canonical commit/outbox, recursive
  evidence promotion, and wallet local/remote backup state machines; replay is
  idempotent and no side-state can roll back or fabricate canonical finality.
- Recursive-evidence scenarios complete proof and archive-availability lanes in
  both orders and expose the epoch manifest/evidence/PQ anchors only after the
  generation/root-bound join and initial retrieval audit succeed.
- The next available certified epoch close commits the prior evidence anchor's
  typed evidence-MMR inclusion; before that point the anchor is local pending
  and cannot authorize network promotion or retention deletion.
- Tamper/reorg/restart/resource/archive/audit/snapshot/pruning/non-authority
  scenarios pass, including actual Nova and Plonky3 verifier rejection under
  changed network, genesis, config, and predicate context.
- The shared context replay matrix contains exactly eight mandatory rows:
  `CTX-NETWORK-{NOVA,PLONKY3}`, `CTX-GENESIS-{NOVA,PLONKY3}`,
  `CTX-CONFIG-{NOVA,PLONKY3}`, and
  `CTX-PREDICATE-{NOVA,PLONKY3}`. Each row must reach the corresponding actual
  pinned verifier and record typed rejection independently of envelope checks.
- Every additional shared predicate/witness mutation declared by
  `.planning/phases/069-Recursive-Proof/069-PREDICATE.md` has one Nova and one Plonky3 execution row; missing or
  duplicate backend rows fail the scenario audit.
- The inherited-live distributed HJMT matrix is mandatory: replication, quorum,
  conflict resolution, standby catch-up, route rollout, dispatch, membership,
  restart, partition/heal, stale lineage, divergent roots, and failure telemetry
  each execute through existing components and persist one typed evidence
  pointer. Its positive profile has exactly 16 complete non-overlapping shards,
  three distinct-domain replicas/write quorum two per shard, no full-state
  placement, and one global state root. `HJMT-ROUTE-ROLLOUT` includes COW copy,
  root verification, ordered delta catch-up and canonical CAS; the other eleven
  rows cross-check that active route generation.
- Cadence failures translate exactly to `HybridCadenceMismatch` and
  `PqAnchorMissing`. `PqAnchorMissing` applies to recursive-evidence promotion
  or pruning authorization after epoch closure; it MUST NOT reject the
  canonical height-1000 transition.
- Wallet-boundary scenario gives a wallet only `FinalizedWalletReceiptV2`
  network trust anchor, committee keyset/threshold/rotation manifest,
  certificate/generation, committed root, transaction/owned-output
  inclusion, HJMT membership, epoch anchor, and expected output fields; it
  proves ownership without PP, PK, VK, or a recursive sidecar.
  A separately enabled recursive-verifier scenario caches the generation-pinned
  VK once and never receives prover material. Ordinary validator, watcher, and
  wallet scenarios receive no PP/PK/VK.
- The simulator suite emits and reloads the required 3-step/5-step sidecars,
  real Nova and Plonky3 proof evidence, tampered-middle and tampered-witness
  rejects, canonical-admission invariance, local-spike measurements, cadence
  and PQ-anchor evidence, mandatory measured RS(10,16) archive-shard receipts
  with no full-replica fallback, unpinned-object rejection, sharded snapshot/
  current-state/seed-recovery evidence, authorized
  before/after pruning decisions, retention plateau, wallet restore, and all
  three crash matrices.
- A registry/cutover scenario enumerates every registered old/new object row and
  the invalid Cartesian product across type ID, wire codec, crypto-domain,
  public-input/root encoding, and authority/parameter generation. Only declared
  tuples pass; suffix dispatch, downgrade, fallback, and mixed-era recovery fail
  before allocation, verifier invocation, or durable mutation.
