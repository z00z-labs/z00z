<objective>

every block, verify real 3-step and 5-step chains, and make accumulator snapshots
restartable and fork-safe without conflating this accumulator recovery with the
multi-artifact job transaction owned by `069-10`.

## acceptance_tests

- Real 3-step and 5-step chains verify with prior-output and Nova accumulator continuity.
- Restart resumes the same accumulator; reorg/stale predecessor/mixed parameter/corruption rejects or quarantines.
- A wrong middle-step statement digest, missing intermediate measurement, or
  mismatched Nova proof-digest root returns the exact shared typed reason and
  identifies the failing chain index.
- Ordinary canonical validators receive no PP/PK/VK or per-block Nova proof;
  an explicit recursive-verifier role fetches one authority-pinned VK per

  encoding/parameter generations reject before recovery state changes.

## cadence_and_publication_contract

The implementation intent is continuous local folding with sparse durable and
network artifacts:

- `fold_cadence_blocks` MUST equal `1` and MUST NOT be runtime-, witness-,
  proof-, wallet-, or peer-selected.
- `recovery_snapshot_cadence_blocks` SHOULD start at `100` as the planning
  candidate. Plan 11 measurements MAY select another positive value, but the
  selected value MUST be authority-pinned, versioned, and recorded before use.
- `compression_cadence_blocks` and `publication_cadence_blocks` SHOULD default
  to the configured `1000`-block network epoch. They MAY differ only when
  measured evidence records the operational reason and Plan 12 proves all
  boundary behavior. Neither may silently inherit the fold or recovery interval.
- `on_demand` compression/publication MAY be requested by an authorized local
  operator or recovery workflow, but it MUST NOT consume/reset the running
  accumulator, change public endpoints, or become an attacker-controlled
  unbounded job source.
- Publication MUST be content-addressed pull/announce: consensus traffic carries
  at most a bounded digest/reference. Full proof bytes go only to explicit
  recursive verifiers. Per-block proof broadcast is prohibited by default.
- The hot recovery set MUST be bounded to the active accumulator, exactly the
  two most recent verified recovery snapshots, the current/previous generation
  manifests, and the explicitly bounded pending-epoch queue. Superseded or stale
  forks are quarantined and later deleted through Plan 10's journaled local-
  recovery GC after rollback/reader references clear. They are not Nova proof
  bodies and MUST NOT be addressed by a Plan 09 proof-body retention ticket.
- The hot recovery set also requires a positive finite byte cap selected from
  Plan 11 measurements before production activation. The planning ConfigV3 value
  `max_nova_hot_recovery_bytes: 0` is fail-closed/measurement-pending, not
  unlimited. Count-only bounds, host-RAM-derived caps, or reuse of the 24-GiB RSS
  ceiling as a disk cap reject.
- Each accepted scheduled/on-demand compression MUST emit immutable
  epoch/range/body digest, Nova chain root, parameter/authority/profile generation,
  and reference facts to the sole Plan 09 retention ledger. Retries and temporary
  bodies are deleted locally before acceptance and MUST NOT expand Plan 09's
  configured two-body-per-epoch retained set.
- A cadence change is a generation/config transition. It MUST bind chain context,
  activation height, prior/new cadence manifests, parameter generation, and
  rollback bounds; mixed-cadence proof chains reject.
