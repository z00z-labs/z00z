## version_boundary_contract

- Plan 07 MUST consume `CheckpointVersionRegistryV2` and register every
  persisted base-proof/receipt/profile artifact. A private trace/AIR subrecord
  MAY use local codec version 1 only when the selected grammar/profile digest
  binds it; that local choice is not public wire-V1 compatibility.
- Backend construction MUST bind the activated ConfigV3 digest/generation.
  ConfigV2 is offline migration input only and MUST NOT select a proof grammar,
  transcript, cap, parameter set, or decoder after activation.
- Before parameters or transcript state are constructed, ConfigV3 and the
  registry MUST select the same runtime profile identifier, generation, and
  authority-pinned canonical manifest digest. Reusing an identifier/generation
  with altered manifest bytes/digest MUST reject as profile substitution.
- The fixed artifact preheader selects the exact type, wire codec, cap, and
  activated authority generation before allocation. The verifier profile then
  selects the cryptographic-domain, transcript, public-input encoding, AIR,
  hash, FRI, and parameter generations. No integer equality or `V2` suffix may
  substitute for those labeled binds.
- The actual Fiat-Shamir transcript and verification receipt MUST bind the
  registry digest and exact runtime-profile-manifest digest. Unknown, cross-
  type, downgraded, cross-generation,
  or post-activation-old writes MUST reject; decode or verifier failure MUST
  NOT fall back to another codec or parameter set.
- Registry completeness, old/new Cartesian decoder/verifier vectors, wrong-
  profile mutations, and activation-boundary tests MUST be release gates.

## base_proof_lifecycle_contract

- The AIR MUST prove the storage-owned transition predicate independently. A
  Nova receipt, Nova proof digest, or equality of public roots MAY be auxiliary
  differential evidence but MUST NOT satisfy any Plonky3 constraint.
- Canonical serialization MUST be finite and versioned; parsers MUST enforce all
  nested counts and byte caps before allocation or hashing and reject trailing,
  duplicate, non-canonical, wrong-endian, or field-alias encodings.
- The transcript MUST absorb the complete domain/version/role, chain context,
  predicate and AIR digests, trace shape, all public inputs, MMCS/PCS/FRI/hash
  parameter digests, and commitments before challenges. No commit-then-reveal
  field may remain outside the transcript.
- FRI security MUST be derived from the concrete field/extension, blowup,
  queries, grinding, hash assumptions, recursion depth, and composition loss.
  The configured security-bit literal alone is not evidence.
- Soundness bounds MUST be encoded canonically as upward-rounded dyadic
  rationals and composed with checked integer arithmetic; floating point,
  wraparound, downward rounding, or a parameter-rotation reset is forbidden.
  The manifest MUST bind parameter generation, derivation inputs, per-proof
  bound, finite accepted-epoch budget, inherited bound, composition rule, and
  minimum residual target for Plan 08.
- Base proof bodies SHOULD be deleted after Plan 08 has atomically committed and
  re-verified the enclosing epoch proof and its recovery window has elapsed.
  Their digests remain in the epoch manifest/challenge pack for the bounded
  Plan 09 window. Ordinary validators and wallets MUST NOT download them.
- Any selected `p3-*` revision, parameter set, or transcript change creates a new
  generation and requires complete positive, differential, mutation, and
  cross-generation rejection evidence.
